My rating: <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/6829869375">5 of 5 stars</a><br /><br />
The book is a relic of times gone by, but it offers tons of wisdom and is a useful guide on how to start being nicer to people you meet in everyday situations.<br /><br />​My point for reading this book was not to influence people, but even the points on that topic are great for becoming a better conversationalist and a kinder human being. This part, for example, tackles how to give negative feedback and situations leaders often face.<br /><br />​All in all, this was a book about being a good leader and a good friend, and I like that. It's not a guidebook to live by (as others have said in the reviews), but there are some amazing points and ideas (or rather reminders) which help you become a better human being and make other people happier - which makes you happier in turn.
