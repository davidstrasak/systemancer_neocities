My rating: <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/6800874443">1 of 5 stars</a><br /><br />
This book is <b>so old</b>.<br /><br />At first I didn't realise how old it is, because I bought the book in a bookstore on a whim, but just so you know - the book was last updated by Graham in 1972 and this version has commentary by Jason Zweig that was written in 2003.<br /><br />At the time of writing this, it's been 21 years of no updates. So the book does not take in the fact that:<br />- You are able to trade without commissions from your phone.<br />- You can trade using algorithms.<br />- You won't know most of the companies' names, because they went bankrupt already.<br />- I believe there are better ways to price investments since then.<br /><br />My other gripes with the book are:<br />- It's really long and slow to read.<br />- Graham's style of english (old english).<br /><br />So all in all, while the book did not change, the market did change, and it did a lot. If you're thinking about picking this book up, I'd suggest going for a newer book. I'm sure there could be some book that's 300 pages shorter, teaches you all the same principles, and there isn't such a desparity between technologies of today and back then.<br />Alternatively you can search for the main points from the book and save time and money.
