My rating: <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/7693955255">3 of 5 stars</a><br /><br />
This is a gamebook that is really cool in terms of the story and the world. There is a lot of choice, there are interesting plot developments and any way you go there will be interesting bits of action or conversation.<br /><br />The mechanics, however, drag it down hard. The rules are just way too bloated. It might be interesting to some, but I like rules light games and I had problems trying to do everything I was supposed to. At first I solved this by skipping the ranged mech fighting phase, but even that was too much, so I resulted to resolving every conflict as a victory without fighting.<br /><br />So you have these bloated and pretty dificult mechanics and now: add the map of Tokyo. Congrats, you now have a game that is hard af to play :D.<br /><br />At some points I have also found issues with some game design decisions that were confusing - stuff like mixing the DNA bomb, which I still don't understand how I was supposed to mix it (even though you can find the correct solution by just trying all the mixes).<br /><br />The gamebook is pretty good, but definitely not my style. That's why I'm giving it a 3/5 score.
